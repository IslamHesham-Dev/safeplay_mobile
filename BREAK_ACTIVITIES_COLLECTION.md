# Break Activities & Mindful Games Collection

## Collection Name: `breakActivities`

This collection stores mindful and fun break activities designed for children to use during break times that teachers can schedule.

### Purpose

These activities are designed to:
- **Promote emotional wellbeing**: Help children manage stress and emotions
- **Provide mental breaks**: Allow children to rest their minds between learning activities
- **Encourage mindfulness**: Teach calming and focus techniques
- **Support creativity**: Offer creative expression opportunities
- **Build resilience**: Develop emotional awareness and regulation skills

### Age Group Differences

#### Junior (6-8) - Simple & Playful
- **Simple activities**: Easy to understand and follow
- **Playful approach**: Activities feel like games, not exercises
- **Visual/Interactive**: Strong visual elements and simple interactions
- **Short duration**: 2-5 minutes typically
- **High engagement**: Fun and immediately engaging

#### Bright (9-12) - Sophisticated & Reflective
- **More complex**: Activities with deeper engagement
- **Self-reflective**: Encourage introspection and self-awareness
- **Creative expression**: More opportunities for personal expression
- **Longer duration**: 3-7 minutes typically
- **Choice-based**: Children can choose their activities

### Activity Types

#### 1. **Breathing Activities**
- **Junior**: Bubble Breathing (simple visual breathing)
- **Bright**: Breathing Challenge (box breathing technique)

#### 2. **Coloring & Creative Activities**
- **Junior**: Color the Mandala (simple coloring)
- **Bright**: Mandala Coloring Challenge (complex patterns with color schemes)

#### 3. **Movement & Yoga**
- **Junior**: Animal Yoga Poses (fun animal poses)
- **Bright**: Mindful Walking (guided mindful movement)

#### 4. **Mindfulness & Meditation**
- **Junior**: Nature Sounds Listening (simple listening)
- **Bright**: Guided Meditation Journey (visualization and meditation)

#### 5. **Emotional Awareness**
- **Junior**: Feelings Faces Game (recognizing emotions)
- **Bright**: Emotion Check-in Wheel (exploring and naming emotions)

#### 6. **Gratitude Exercises**
- **Junior**: Gratitude Drawing (draw what makes you happy)
- **Bright**: Gratitude Journal (written gratitude practice)

#### 7. **Music & Rhythm**
- **Junior**: Musical Rhythm Tap (simple rhythm following)
- **Bright**: Beat Maker Studio (create your own beats)

#### 8. **Puzzles & Challenges**
- **Junior**: Animal Friends Match (simple matching game)
- **Bright**: Puzzle Challenge (Sudoku, Word Search, Crosswords)

#### 9. **Creative Expression**
- **Junior**: Happy Dance Party (free movement)
- **Bright**: Creative Story Starters (writing prompts)

#### 10. **Relaxation Techniques**
- **Junior**: Body Scan Relaxation (simple body awareness)
- **Bright**: Progressive Muscle Relaxation (advanced relaxation)

### Collection Structure

Each document includes:
- **Basic Info**: id, title, type, prompt, instructions
- **Activity Details**: activityType, gameType, estimatedTimeSeconds
- **Educational**: skills, topics, points, difficultyLevel
- **Metadata**: learningObjective, emotionalGoal, prerequisiteSkills
- **Status**: isActive, isBreakActivity, createdAt, updatedAt

### Gamification Elements

- **Points**: Awarded for participation and completion (15-35 points)
- **Skills**: Tracked for wellbeing progress (breathing, calm, creativity, etc.)
- **Achievements**: Unlock special rewards for regular break activity participation
- **Streak Tracking**: Encourage daily break activity practice
- **Progress Visualization**: Show emotional growth over time

### Teacher Control

Teachers can:
- **Schedule break times**: Set when break activities are available
- **Assign activities**: Choose specific activities for break times
- **Track participation**: See which children engage with break activities
- **Monitor wellbeing**: Observe patterns in activity choices
- **Customize**: Add or remove activities based on classroom needs

### Usage in Child Accounts

During break times:
1. Child sees available break activities
2. Child chooses an activity (or teacher assigns)
3. Child completes the activity
4. Child earns points and tracks skills
5. Activity contributes to overall wellbeing score
